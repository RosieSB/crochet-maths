## Miscounts and mismeasurement (for science)
Failure to meet the requirement $h=\frac{r\pi}{N}$ and/or mis-measurement of stitch sizes results in geometric "torusoids" (surfaces of revolution of non-circular ellipses). This appears to be most noticeable when $R/r$ is close to $1$.

As can be seen in [](fig:7-colour-2), Attempt 2 (right) resulted in a less distorted torus than attempt 1 (left), and with more circular cross-sections. This illustrates the difficulty of measuring stitch dimensions from only one stitch. Better choices for w and h were calculated by measuring the two characteristic circumferences of the Attempt 1 crocheted torus and dividing by number of rows/stitches, as appropriate.
