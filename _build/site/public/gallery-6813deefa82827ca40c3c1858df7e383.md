## Miscounts and mismeasurements (for science)
Failure to meet the requirement $h=\frac{r\pi}{N}$ and/or mis-measurement of stitch sizes results in geometric "torusoids" (surfaces of revolution of non-circular ellipses). This appears to be most noticeable when $R/r$ is close to $1$. 

### Feb 2026
:::{figure} figs/Feb26_torus_1.jpg
:width: 400
:label: fig:Feb26_torus_1
$R=4$, $r=1.6$, $w=0.4$, $h=0.4$, $N=20$, $\frac{r\pi}{N}=0.251<h$
:::

This is a simple case of forgetting to update the row count, as I will still using a spreadsheet. $N=12$ or $N=13$ would have worked better. I also suspect I mismeasured the stitch dimensions.
