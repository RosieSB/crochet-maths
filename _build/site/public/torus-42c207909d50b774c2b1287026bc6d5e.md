# Torus
Consider a torus embedded in $\mathbb{R}^3$, centred on the origin and with major and minor radii $R>r>0$.

:::{figure} figs/torus-diagram.png
:width: 400
:label: fig:torus-diag

Diagram of a torus with radii $R>r>0$.
:::

A corresponding parametrisation is 

\begin{gather*}
\mathbf{r}:[-\pi,\pi]\times[0,2\pi]\to\mathbb{R}^3; \hspace{1em} \mathbf{r}(\theta,\phi)=(x,y,z) \\
x=(R-r\cos\theta)\cos\phi, \hspace{1em} y=(R-r\cos\theta)\sin\phi, \hspace{1em} z=r\sin\theta
\end{gather*}

We measure crochet rows in units of $\theta$, and begin with the upper half ($z\geq 0$, $0\leq\theta\leq\pi$). The lower half ($z<0$) is identical and can be added on after. 

By design, the first row corresponds to $\theta = 0$ and has circumference $2\pi(R-r)$. Assume a constant gauge, with each stitch measuring $w$ wide and $h$ tall. Then, the starting round should have approximately $2\pi(R-r)/w$ stitches.

Let $N$ denote the total number of rows for the upper half, **chosen so as to satisfy $N\approx\frac{r\pi}{h}$**.  

For each $n$, write $C(n)$ for the circumference of row $n$ and write $s(n)$ for the stitch count of row $n$. Then, for $n=0,1,\ldots, N$,
$$
s(n) = \frac{C(n)}{w},
$$
and
$$
C(n)=2\pi\left(R-r\cos\left(\frac{n\pi}{N}\right)\right).
$$

## Torus crochet 

**Set up:** Chain $\left\lfloor\frac{2\pi(R-r)}{w}\right\rfloor$ stitches and join into a round.

**Row n:** Crochet $\left\lfloor \frac{2\pi}{w}\left(R-r\cos\left(\frac{n\pi}{N}\right)\right)\right\rfloor$ stitches around. Taking care to spread increases as evenly as possible.

### Example

:::{aside}
[See here](#interactive-torus) for an interactive version.
:::

:::{code-cell} python
:label: parameters
:tags: [remove-input]
import numpy as np

# Major & minor radii
R = 4
r = 1.78
# Stitch dimensions
w = .4
h = .4
# Row count
N = round(r*np.pi/h)
# Stitch count
st_count = [0]*(N+1)
row = [0]*(N+1)
for n in range(N+1):
    theta = n*np.pi/N
    st_count[n] = str(round(2*np.pi*(R-r*np.cos(theta))/w))
    row[n] = n #"Row "+str(n)+": "+str(st_count[n])+" st."
:::

Parameters: major radius R = {eval}`R`; minor radius r = {eval}`r`; stitch height h = {eval}`h`; stitch width w = {eval}`w`. 

Number of rows: $N = \lfloor\frac{r\pi}{h}\rfloor = ${eval}`N`.

**Row 0.** Chain {eval}`st_count[0]` and join into round. 1 st into each chain sp around.

**Subsequent rows:** Work the following stitch counts in subsequent rows. Increases made using 2 st in st of previous row. Try to evenly distribute increases for a symmetrical look.

:::{code-cell}
:label: pattern-table
:tags: [remove-input]
from tabulate import tabulate
import numpy as np

table = np.transpose([row, st_count])
pattern = tabulate(table,headers=["Row","Stitch count"],tablefmt="html")
display(pattern)
:::

This completes the top half of the torus. The bottom half is almost identical - just turn the work upside down and repeat, omitting the very last row (row {eval}`N`). 

## Geometry of the torus

### Riemannian metric and Christoffel symbols

We have
$$
\mathbf{r}=\big((R-r\cos\theta)\cos\phi,(R-r\cos\theta)\sin\phi,r\sin\theta\big)
$$
so,
\begin{gather*}
\mathbf{r}_\theta=\left(r\sin\theta\cos\phi,r\sin\theta\sin\phi,r\cos\theta\right), \\
\mathbf{r}_\phi=\left(-(R-r\cos\theta)\sin\phi,(R-r\cos\theta)\cos\phi,0\right), \\
\mathbf{r}_{\theta\theta}=\left(r\cos\theta\cos\phi,r\cos\theta\sin\phi,-r\sin\theta\right),\\
\mathbf{r}_{\theta\phi}=\left(-r\sin\theta\sin\phi,r\sin\theta\cos\phi,0\right), \text{ and }\\
\mathbf{r}_{\phi\phi}=\left(-(R-r\cos\theta)\cos\phi,-(R-r\cos\theta)\sin\phi,0\right).
\end{gather*}

Hence,
$$
\mathbf{r}_\theta\times\mathbf{r}_\phi=\left(\begin{array}{c} 
-r(R-r\cos\theta)\cos\theta\cos\phi\\
-r(R-r\cos\theta)\cos\theta\sin\phi\\
r\sin\theta(R-r\cos\theta)
\end{array}\right),
$$
and so 
$$
|\mathbf{r}_\theta\times\mathbf{r}_\phi|^2=r^2(R-r\cos\theta)^2.
$$

From these we can calculate everything else. 

#### Unit normal

$$
\hat{\mathbf{n}}=\frac{\mathbf{r}_\theta\times\mathbf{r}_\phi}{|\mathbf{r}_\theta\times\mathbf{r}_\phi|}=\left(-\cos\theta\cos\phi,-\cos\theta\sin\phi,\sin\theta\right).
$$

#### First and second fundamental forms
$$
E=\mathbf{r}_\theta^2=r^2, \hspace{1em} F=\mathbf{r}_\theta\cdot\mathbf{r}_\phi=0 \hspace{1em} G=\mathbf{r}_\phi^2=(R-r\cos\theta)^2
$$
$$
L=\mathbf{r}_{\theta\theta}\cdot\hat{\mathbf{n}}=-r,\hspace{1em}
M=\mathbf{r}_{\theta\phi}\cdot\hat{\mathbf{n}}=0, \hspace{1em} N=\mathbf{r}_{\phi\phi}\cdot\hat{\mathbf{n}}=(R-r\cos\theta)\cos\theta.
$$
So, the first and second fundamental forms are
$$
\mathbf{I}=r^2d\theta^2 + (R-r\cos\theta)^2d\phi, \hspace{.5em}\text{ and }\hspace{.5em}\mathbf{II}=-r\,d\theta^2+(R-r\cos\theta)\cos\theta\,d\phi^2.
$$

#### Metric tensor
$$
g=(g_{ij})=\left(\begin{array}{cc}r^2 & 0 \\ 0 & (R-r\cos\theta)^2 \end{array}\right),
$$ 

We also use its inverse
$$
g^{-1}=(g^{ij})=\left(\begin{array}{cc}\frac{1}{r^2} & 0 \\ 0 & \frac{1}{(R-r\cos\theta)^2} \end{array}\right)
$$

#### Christoffel symbols
The usual definition 
:::{math}
:enumerated: true
:label: eq:christoffel
\Gamma^i_{kl}=\frac{1}{2}g^{im}\left(\partial_lg_{mk}+\partial_kg_{ml}+\partial_mg_{kl}\right)
:::
yields
$$
\Gamma^\theta_{\phi\theta}=\Gamma^\theta_{\theta\phi}=\frac{r\sin\theta}{R-r\cos\theta},\hspace{1em}\Gamma^{\theta}_{\phi\phi}=-\frac{1}{r}(R-\cos\theta)\sin\theta, \hspace{1em} \Gamma^\theta_{\theta\theta}=\Gamma^\phi_{\theta\theta}=\Gamma^\phi_{\theta\phi}=\Gamma^\phi_{\phi\theta}=0.
$$

:::{note}
These calculations are lengthy and I omit them for now. They are analogous to those in [these notes by Mark L. Irons](http://www.rdrop.com/~half/math/torus/torus.geodesics.pdf). The above equations can be recovered using $u=\phi$, $v=\theta$, and the [Christoffel reparametrisation formula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christoffel_symbols#Transformation_law_under_change_of_variable).

Or, you can enjoy verifying them directly for yourself using [](#eq:christoffel).
:::

### Curvature
#### Principle curvatures:
These are the roots $\kappa_1,\kappa_2$ of  the quadratic equation
$$
\det(\mathbf{II}-\kappa\mathbf{I})=\left(\begin{array}{cc} L-\kappa E & M-\kappa F \\ M-\kappa F & N-\kappa G\end{array}\right)=(L-\kappa E)(N-\kappa G) = 0.
$$
In other words, 
$$
\kappa_1=\frac{L}{E}=-\frac{1}{r}, \hspace{1em} \kappa_2=\frac{N}{G}=\frac{\cos\theta}{R-r\cos\theta}.
$$

#### Gauss curvature:
$$
K=\kappa_1\kappa_2=-\frac{\cos\theta}{r(R-r\cos\theta)}.
$$

#### Mean curvature
$$
H=\frac{\kappa_1+\kappa_2}{2}=\frac{2r\cos\theta-R}{r(R-r\cos\theta)}.
$$


#### Curvature tensor
All equal $0$ except 
$$
R^\theta_{\phi\theta\phi}=-R^\theta_{\phi\phi\theta}=-\frac{1}{r}\cos\theta(R-r\cos\theta),
$$
and
$$
R^\phi_{\theta\phi\theta}=-R^\phi_{\theta\theta\phi}=-\frac{\cos\theta}{R-r\cos\theta}
$$

#### Ricci  curvature tensor
Contracting the curvature tensor according to $R_{ij}=R^m_{imj}$ gives
$$
R_{\theta\theta}=R^\theta_{\theta\theta\theta}+R^\phi_{\theta\phi\theta}=-\frac{1}{r}\cos\theta(R-r\cos\theta), \hspace{1em} R_{\phi\phi}=R^\theta_{\phi\theta\phi}+R^\phi_{\phi\phi\phi}=-\frac{\cos\theta}{R-r\cos\theta}
$$

### Geodesics
This parametrisation is an example of a *Clairaut parametrisation*, in the sense that the metric tensor $g$ only depends on $\theta$ and not $\phi$. This means geodesics have a particularly nice form.

Suppose $\gamma(t)=\mathbf{r}(\theta(t),\phi(t))$ is a unit speed geodesic on the torus. Then (c.f. Theorem 5.3 of O'Neill, pp. 354) 
$$
c=G\dot{\theta}=\sqrt{G}\sin(\alpha)
$$
is constant along $\gamma$, where $\alpha$ is the angle made with $\theta$. So,
$$
c=(R-r\cos\theta)^2\dot\phi=(R-r\cos\theta)\sin\alpha \tag{$*$}
$$ is constant along $\gamma$, and moreover (c.f. O'Neill Prop 5.5, pp. 357),
$$
\frac{d\phi}{d\theta}=\pm\frac{c\sqrt{E}}{\sqrt G\sqrt{G-c^2}}=\pm\frac{rc}{{(R-r\cos\theta)}\sqrt{(R-r\cos\theta)^2-c^2}}
$$
$$
	\phi = \phi_0\pm cr\int_{\theta_0}^\theta\frac{dt}{{(R-r\cos t)}\sqrt{(R-r\cos t)^2-c^2}}
$$
By $(*)$, 
$$\dot\phi=\frac{c}{(R-r\cos\theta)^2}, \hspace{1em} \dot\theta=\frac{d\theta}{d\phi}\dot\phi=\pm\frac{\sqrt{(R-r\cos\theta)^2-c^2}}{r(R-r\cos\theta)}
$$
